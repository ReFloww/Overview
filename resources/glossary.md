# Glossary

Key terms and definitions used in ReFlow.

## A

### APY (Annual Percentage Yield)
The total return on an investment over one year, including compound interest.

### ABI (Application Binary Interface)
A standard interface for interacting with smart contracts on Ethereum-compatible blockchains.

## B

### Blockchain
A distributed, immutable ledger that records transactions across multiple computers.

### Borrower
An individual or entity that receives a loan and agrees to repay with interest.

## C

### Collateral
Assets pledged by a borrower to secure a loan.

### Contract Address
A unique identifier for a smart contract on the blockchain.

### Custodial
A system where a third party holds and controls assets on behalf of users.

## D

### DeFi (Decentralized Finance)
Financial services built on blockchain technology without traditional intermediaries.

### Default
Failure of a borrower to meet loan repayment obligations.

## E

### ERC-20
A standard interface for fungible tokens on Ethereum-compatible blockchains.

### Escrow
A financial arrangement where a third party holds funds until conditions are met.

## F

### Factory Contract
A smart contract that deploys other contracts with standardized parameters.

### Funded
A loan that has received the full requested investment amount.

### Fund Manager
A professional who manages investment portfolios on behalf of investors.

## G

### Gas
The fee paid to process transactions on the blockchain.

### Gas Fee
The cost of executing a transaction or smart contract operation.

## H

### Hash
A unique cryptographic identifier generated from data.

## I

### Interest Rate
The percentage charged on a loan, typically expressed annually.

### Investment Token
An ERC-20 token representing ownership in a loan investment.

## K

### KYC (Know Your Customer)
Identity verification process required for regulatory compliance.

## L

### Layer 2 (L2)
A secondary blockchain network that operates on top of a main blockchain for improved scalability.

### Lender
An individual or entity that provides capital for loans in exchange for interest.

### Liquidity
The ease with which an asset can be converted to cash or traded.

### Loan Token
An ERC-20 token that represents a share of investment in a specific loan.

## M

### Managed Mode
Investment mode where fund managers make investment decisions on behalf of investors.

### Mantle Network
A Layer 2 scaling solution for Ethereum, used by ReFlow for low-cost transactions.

### Marketplace
The platform interface where available loans are listed for investment.

### Maturity
The date when a loan is due to be fully repaid.

### MNT
The native token of Mantle Network, used for gas fees.

## N

### Non-Custodial
A system where users maintain control of their own assets and private keys.

## O

### OJK (Otoritas Jasa Keuangan)
Indonesia's Financial Services Authority, the regulator for P2P lending.

### On-Chain
Data or transactions recorded directly on the blockchain.

## P

### P2P Lending (Peer-to-Peer Lending)
Direct lending between individuals without traditional financial intermediaries.

### Portfolio
A collection of investments held by an investor.

### Principal
The original amount of a loan, excluding interest.

### Private Key
A secret cryptographic key that controls access to a blockchain wallet.

## R

### Repayment
Return of borrowed funds plus interest to lenders.

### Restock.id
An OJK-licensed P2P lending platform in Indonesia, ReFlow's lending partner.

### RPC (Remote Procedure Call)
A protocol for communicating with blockchain nodes.

## S

### Secondary Market
A marketplace where investors can buy and sell existing loan tokens.

### Self-Managed Mode
Investment mode where investors directly choose which loans to fund.

### Smart Contract
Self-executing code deployed on a blockchain that automatically enforces agreements.

### Stablecoin
A cryptocurrency designed to maintain a stable value, typically pegged to fiat currency.

## T

### Tenor
The duration or term length of a loan.

### Token
A digital asset created and managed on a blockchain.

### Tokenization
The process of converting assets or rights into blockchain tokens.

### Transaction Hash (TxHash)
A unique identifier for a blockchain transaction.

## U

### USDT (Tether)
A stablecoin pegged to the US dollar, used for investments on ReFlow.

## W

### Wallet
Software or hardware that stores private keys and enables blockchain transactions.

### WalletConnect
A protocol for connecting mobile wallets to decentralized applications.

### Web3
The decentralized internet built on blockchain technology.

## Y

### Yield
The income return on an investment, usually expressed as a percentage.
